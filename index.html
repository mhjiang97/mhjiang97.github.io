Pok Fo Lam, Hong Kong

I am a first-year Ph.D. student at School of Biomedical Sciences, Li Ka Shing Faculty of medicine, The University of Hong Kong majoring in Bioinformatics. My current research focuses on the identification of novel structural variants to subtype patients with B-cell acute lymphoblastic leukemia based on long-read sequencing, advised by Dr Jason Wong.

Prior to joining HKU, I obtained my Bachelor of Science degree in Bioinformatics from Harbin Medical University in 2019. Subsequently, I pursued my Master of Science degree in Biology at Shanghai Institute of Hematology, Ruijin Hospital, School of Medicine, Shanghai Jiao Tong University in 2022, doing research about deciphering the mechanism of leukemogenesis across various subtypes (e.g., DUX4-IGH and MEF2D fusions). Following that, I worked as a technician for 10 months at Shanghai Institute of Immunology, where my responsibilities included sequencing platform construction.
